My goal in life is to have a lasting impact on the sustained increase of human presence amongst the stars. Not only is it inevitable, but it's pretty darn cool.

I do love to program, and I also enjoy amateur astronomy. Observing the planets is always breathtaking (they seem so close!), and it's fun to brush up on the latest astronomy publications. Keeping up with spaceflight and learning about rocket propulsion take up some of my time, too.

Outside of space and programming-related things, I like playing the piano, amateur microscopy, and studying Japanese language.

My Work Experience

May 2019 - Present

Jacobs Technology (NASA TOSC)

Software Engineer

In the Launch Control Center at Kennedy Space Center, I work in a small team on an advanced telemetry tool that is used to simulate vehicle telemetry, which in turn is used to rigorously test the Launch Control System. My work has ranged from adding new simulator functionality to simulate the entire mission profile, to creating a package that creates and interfaces with our telemetry schema database.

September 2018 - May 2019

National Aeronautics and Space Administration

Software Engineer Intern

I worked as part of a UCF team working with Swamp Works at Kennedy Space Center to develop a smaller, more modular version of their lunar excavation robot, the RASSOR.
Programming the driver station desktop application and putting together the simulation were really exciting projects to work on, and I learned the basics of robot control systems.

May 2016 - August 2016

Lockheed Martin

Systems Engineer Intern

At Rotary and Mission Systems, I worked closely with software engineers developing a training simulator for the BAE Systems Hawk T2. I installed and configured flight simulation set-ups, maintained installation and testing documentation, and built a test suite for the newly-developed simulation functions. I became real familiar with the MFDs of the Hawk, particularly the TCAS.
After executing simulation tests and reporting my findings directly to the engineers, seeing fixes and changes in the updated versions gave me a real sense of involvement in the development lifecycle.
